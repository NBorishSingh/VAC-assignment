About the M1 Garand

The M1 Garand is a semi-automatic rifle that served as the standard infantry rifle of the United States during World War II and the Korean War. It was designed by John C. Garand and adopted by the U.S. Army in 1936.

It was the first semi-automatic rifle to be widely issued to soldiers, providing faster firing capability compared to bolt-action rifles used previously.

History

The development of the M1 Garand began in the 1920s. After years of testing, the rifle was officially adopted in 1936 by the United States Army.

  • Adopted in 1936
  • Used in World War II
  • Used in the Korean War
  • Over 5 million units produced
  • Later replaced by the M14 rifle
