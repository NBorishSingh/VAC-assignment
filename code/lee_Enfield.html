About

The Lee–Enfield rifle was a bolt-action service rifle used by British and Commonwealth forces. It became one of the most recognized rifles of the early 20th century and remained in service for many decades.

History

  • Adopted in 1895
  • Used extensively during World War I
  • Continued service during World War II
  • Used by British Commonwealth forces
  • Remained in service into the mid-20th century

Specifications

Specification Details
Type Bolt-Action Rifle
Country United Kingdom
Caliber .303 British
Magazine 10 rounds
Effective Range 500 meters
Weight Approximately 4 kg

Key Features

  • Bolt-action firing mechanism
  • Detachable magazine
  • High accuracy
  • Durable wooden stock
  • Reliable battlefield performance

Interesting Facts

  • The Lee–Enfield was widely used during both World Wars.
  • It became one of the longest-serving military rifles.
  • Many surplus rifles were later used for training.
