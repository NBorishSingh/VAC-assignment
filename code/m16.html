About

The M16 is a lightweight, air-cooled, gas-operated rifle developed in the United States. It became the standard service rifle of the U.S. military during the Vietnam War and remains one of the most widely used rifles in modern military history.

History

  • Developed in the late 1950s
  • Entered military service in the 1960s
  • Widely used during the Vietnam War
  • Adopted by many countries worldwide
  • Later evolved into the M4 carbine

Specifications

Specification Details
Type Assault Rifle
Country United States
Designer Eugene Stoner
Caliber 5.56×45mm NATO
Magazine 20–30 rounds
Effective Range 500–550 meters

Key Features

  • Lightweight aluminum construction
  • Gas-operated firing system
  • Detachable box magazine
  • High accuracy at medium ranges
  • Modular design for upgrades

Interesting Facts

  • The M16 replaced older battle rifles in the 1960s.
  • It is known for its lightweight design.
  • Many modern rifles are based on its design.
