About

The Uzi is a compact submachine gun developed in Israel in the mid-20th century. It became widely known for its simple design, compact size, and reliability. The weapon was designed by Uziel Gal and became one of the most recognizable submachine guns in military and security forces worldwide.

History

  • Developed in the late 1940s
  • Entered service in the 1950s
  • Used by military and security forces
  • Exported to many countries worldwide
  • Became widely recognized globally

Specifications

Specification Details
Type Submachine Gun
Country of Origin Israel
Designer Uziel Gal
Caliber 9×19mm
Magazine 25–32 rounds
Effective Range 200 meters
Weight Approximately 3.5 kg

Key Features

  • Compact and lightweight design
  • Simple mechanical system
  • High reliability
  • Detachable box magazine
  • Easy handling in close environments
  • Durable metal construction

Interesting Facts

  • The Uzi became one of the most famous submachine guns worldwide.
  • It was widely used by military and law enforcement agencies.
  • The compact design made it suitable for close-range operations.
  • Several modern versions of the Uzi were later developed.
